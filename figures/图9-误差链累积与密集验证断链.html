Mechanism Summary: From Empirical Observation to Causal Explanation

This figure reveals the causal mechanism behind the defect density scaling law: (a) In autoregressive generation without intermediate verification, error probability compounds along the token chain, yielding Nbugs ∝ N1+α and explaining the super-linear trend. (b) Inserting k dense verification points breaks the error chain into k independent sub-chains, reducing the asymptotic growth of expected defects from super-linear to linear in N. This mechanism underpins our "Formal Defect Ceiling" approach (Research Content 2) and "Compositional Correctness" strategy (Research Content 3).
