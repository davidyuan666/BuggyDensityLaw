Key Finding: Empirical Validation of the "Formal Defect Ceiling"

Across all scale levels, formal verification significantly improved safety property pass rates. On average, the pass rate increased from 43.1% (before) to 63.4% (after) — a 47% relative gain. The improvement is most pronounced at xlarge scale (55% gain), where unverified code exhibits the highest defect density. This supports the core concept: within the coverage scope of formal verification, safety-critical defects are effectively suppressed, demonstrating that verifiers (Z3/NuSMV) serve as an upper bound on defect density for verifiable properties.
